Rifting is a geological process that occurs when a continent splits apart and separates into two or more smaller landmasses.This extensional process sometimes generates a stranded terrance as a fragment of the larger continent, but the underlying mechanisms are not well-understood. Using numerical modelling, we explored the influence of deformation related to an ancient orogeny in the creation of Canada's Nain Province.

Seismic stratigraphy and sedimentation of Lake Simard

Dec 2021

Lake Simard is situated in the western Quebec seismic zone, which is among Canada's most seismically active areas and poses a threat to Ottawa, Quebec, and New York. Our study involved analyzing data from a seismic reflection survey of the Lake Simard basin. We identified two primary stratigraphic successions, originating from the lateglacial and postglacial periods, respectively. We also investigated the extent of seismicity and the potential use of glaciated Shield lakes to study past intraplate earthquakes.

Seismic Surveying in the Valley

July 2021

Seismic surveying is a geophysical technique that employs seismic waves to generate a 3D image of the Earth's subsurface, similar to a CAT scan. As part of my exploration geophysics course, we evaluated the functionality of seismic reflection and refraction surveying equipment. The course curriculum encompassed various surveying techniques, including electromagnetic, gravity, and ground-penetrating radar (GPR). For our experiment, we utilized the hammer seismic method, which involves striking a hammer to create an acoustic signal that penetrates the subsurface. Following the initial strike, geophones are used to capture the reflected seismic waves.

Capturing Glacial Mega-grooves

July 2021

Mega-grooves are geological formations left by glaciers, which provide valuable information about the speed and direction of past ice flows on the bedrock. As a glacier moves, it picks up and carries subglacial material, which acts as an abrasive and can carve into the underlying bedrock. By examining these features, researchers can reconstruct the paleo-glacial dynamics of an area. Our study focused on measuring and documenting mega-grooves near Odessa, Ontario. Our analysis revealed a southwest movement of the Ontario Lobe during the last glacial maximum, as evidenced by the observed patterns. Additionally, the crossing striations suggest a northwest regression during deglaciation.
