Continental Rifting and Breakup Evolution

Sept 2022

Continental rifting is initiated by necking instability, leading to the thinning of the crust, the rupture of the continent, the formation of a mid-ocean ridge, and finally the onset of seafloor spreading. This extensional process sometimes generates a stranded continental terrance as a fragment of the larger craton, but the underlying mechanisms are not well-understood. We explored the influence of lithospheric deformation related to ancient orogenesis of the Torngat Orogen in the origin of the Nain Province continental fragment in Eastern Canada through numerical modelling using finite-element geodynamics code ASPECT.

Seismo-stratigraphy and the sedimentation of Lake Simard

Dec 2021

Lake Simard is situated in the western Quebec seismic zone, one of Canada's most seismically active area that poses risk to the major metropolitan cities of Ottawa, Quebec and New York. In this study, we carried out a seismic reflection survey and collected 32 km of track line data across the Lake Simard basin. We recognized two principal seismo-stratigraphic successions – with lateglacial and postglacial origins, and explored the role of bedrock fractures in controlling sedimentation and the suitability of glaciated Shield lakes for investigation of past intraplate earthquakes.

Seismic Surveying in the Valley

July 2021

Seismic surveying is an applied geophysical method that uses reflected sound waves to produce a “CAT scan” of the Earth's subsurface. We tested seismic reflection and refraction surveying equipment in the valley near the University of Toronto Scarborough, in preparation for an exploration geophysics course that I am taking. The course will also cover electromagnetic, gravity and ground penetrating radar (GPR) surveying techniques. We conducted the seismic surveying using a hammer seismic method, where a hammer is striked to produce acoustic signal that penetrates the subsurface. The reflected seismic waves are then captured by the geophones following the initial strike.

Capturing Glacial Mega-grooves

July 2021

Mega-grooves are glacial geological records of the velocity and dominant direction of past ice flows on the bedrock. As the glacier moves, it collects subglacial materals forming an erosive underbelly capable of carving into the hard bedrock surface. The remnants of this process provide valuable evidence for reconstructing the paleo-glacial dynamics in the region. We measured and captured these mega grooves near Odessa, Ontario. The patterns observed at Odessa describe a southwest progression of the Ontario Lobe during the last glacial maximum, and the crossing striations indicate a northwest regression during deglaciation.
